# ISO/IEC 42001:2023 — Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS)

The international standard for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an AI Management System. Follows the same high-level structure as ISO 27001, enabling integrated management system implementation.

## Annex A: Control Objectives and Controls

### A.2 — Policies Related to AI

| Control ID | Control Name | Description |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| A.2.1 | AI policy | Establish a policy for AI that provides management direction and support, aligned with business objectives and applicable regulations. |
| A.2.2 | AI policy communication | Ensure the AI policy is communicated to relevant internal and external interested parties. |
| A.2.3 | AI policy review | Review the AI policy at planned intervals or when significant changes occur to ensure it remains suitable and effective. |

### A.3 — Internal Organization

| Control ID | Control Name | Description |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| A.3.1 | Roles and responsibilities | Define and assign accountability and responsibility for AI governance, development, deployment, and operation. |
| A.3.2 | AI governance structure | Establish a governance structure that provides oversight of AI activities, including cross-functional representation. |
| A.3.3 | Reporting and communication | Define reporting lines and communication channels for AI-related decisions, incidents, and performance. |
| A.3.4 | Segregation of duties | Separate conflicting duties in AI development and deployment to reduce opportunities for unauthorized use or unintended harm. |

### A.4 — Resources for AI Systems

| Control ID | Control Name | Description |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| A.4.1 | Resource allocation | Identify and allocate resources (human, technical, financial) needed for AI system development and operation. |
| A.4.2 | Data resources | Ensure availability, quality, and suitability of data used for AI system training, validation, and testing. |
| A.4.3 | Computing resources | Provision appropriate computing infrastructure for AI workloads including development, training, and inference. |
| A.4.4 | Human resources and competence | Ensure personnel involved in AI have the necessary skills, knowledge, and training. Includes awareness of ethical AI principles. |
| A.4.5 | Tool and technology management | Select, evaluate, and manage tools and technologies used in AI development and deployment. |

### A.5 — AI System Lifecycle

| Control ID | Control Name | Description |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| A.5.1 | AI system lifecycle planning | Define and document the lifecycle stages for each AI system: planning, design, development, testing, deployment, operation, monitoring, and retirement. |
| A.5.2 | Requirements specification | Document functional and non-functional requirements for AI systems, including performance, fairness, transparency, and safety requirements. |
| A.5.3 | Design and architecture | Design AI systems following principles of responsible AI, including explainability, robustness, and human oversight provisions. |
| A.5.4 | Development and implementation | Implement AI systems using secure development practices, version control, and documented procedures. |
| A.5.5 | Verification and validation | Test AI systems against requirements using appropriate methods (unit, integration, acceptance testing, adversarial testing). |
| A.5.6 | Deployment | Deploy AI systems following documented deployment procedures, including rollback plans and user notification. |
| A.5.7 | Operation and monitoring | Monitor AI systems in production for performance degradation, bias drift, data drift, and unexpected behavior. |
| A.5.8 | Change management | Manage changes to AI systems through a documented process including impact assessment and approval. |
| A.5.9 | Retirement and decommissioning | Plan and execute the retirement of AI systems including data handling, user notification, and knowledge preservation. |

### A.6 — Data for AI Systems

| Control ID | Control Name | Description |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| A.6.1 | Data management policy | Establish policy governing data acquisition, preparation, labeling, storage, and disposal for AI systems. |
| A.6.2 | Data quality | Define and implement data quality criteria including accuracy, completeness, consistency, timeliness, and relevance. |
| A.6.3 | Data provenance | Maintain records of data origin, transformations, and lineage for training, validation, and testing datasets. |
| A.6.4 | Data preparation | Document data preparation processes including cleaning, normalization, feature engineering, and augmentation. |
| A.6.5 | Data bias assessment | Assess datasets for potential biases that could lead to discriminatory or unfair outcomes. Document findings and mitigation. |
| A.6.6 | Data privacy and consent | Ensure data used in AI systems complies with applicable privacy regulations and consent requirements. |
| A.6.7 | Data security | Protect data used in AI systems against unauthorized access, modification, and destruction throughout the lifecycle. |

### A.7 — Information for Interested Parties

| Control ID | Control Name | Description |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| A.7.1 | Transparency of AI systems | Provide information about AI systems to interested parties including purpose, capabilities, and limitations. |
| A.7.2 | Explainability | Ensure AI system decisions can be explained to affected parties at an appropriate level of detail. |
| A.7.3 | Communication about AI | Communicate clearly about AI system use, including disclosure of AI-generated content and AI-assisted decisions. |
| A.7.4 | Information for users | Provide users with sufficient information to understand how to interact with and interpret AI system outputs. |
| A.7.5 | Notification of AI interaction | Inform individuals when they are interacting with or subject to decisions made by an AI system. |

### A.8 — Use of AI Systems

| Control ID | Control Name | Description |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| A.8.1 | Intended use documentation | Document the intended use, acceptable use boundaries, and known limitations of each AI system. |
| A.8.2 | Responsible use | Establish guidelines for the responsible use of AI systems, including ethical considerations and prohibited uses. |
| A.8.3 | Human oversight | Implement appropriate levels of human oversight for AI systems based on risk assessment, including ability to override. |
| A.8.4 | AI system monitoring | Continuously monitor AI system performance, accuracy, fairness, and safety in production environments. |
| A.8.5 | Incident management | Establish procedures for identifying, reporting, and responding to AI-related incidents and near-misses. |
| A.8.6 | Feedback and complaints | Enable mechanisms for users and affected parties to provide feedback and raise complaints about AI systems. |

### A.9 — Third-Party and Supplier Relationships

| Control ID | Control Name | Description |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| A.9.1 | Third-party AI risk assessment | Assess risks associated with AI systems or components provided by third parties before acquisition or integration. |
| A.9.2 | Supplier agreements | Include AI-specific requirements in supplier agreements covering performance, security, data handling, and liability. |
| A.9.3 | Third-party monitoring | Monitor third-party AI system performance and compliance with agreed requirements throughout the relationship. |
| A.9.4 | Supply chain transparency | Maintain visibility into the AI supply chain including model provenance, training data sources, and component dependencies. |

## Annex B: Implementation Guidance

Annex B provides detailed implementation guidance for each control in Annex A. Key guidance areas:

- **AI Risk Assessment Methodology**: How to identify, analyze, and evaluate AI-specific risks including fairness, safety, transparency, and societal risks
- **Statement of Applicability (SoA)**: Organizations must document which controls are applicable and justify exclusions
- **Integration with ISO 27001**: Controls can be mapped to existing ISMS controls for organizations already certified
- **Metrics and KPIs**: Suggested metrics for measuring AI system performance, fairness, and governance effectiveness

## Annex C: AI Risk Sources

Categories of AI-specific risk that organizations should consider:

| Risk Category | Examples |
|--------------|----------|
| **Bias and fairness** | Discriminatory outcomes, underrepresentation, historical bias amplification |
| **Safety** | Physical harm, critical system failures, autonomous decision errors |
| **Transparency** | Black-box decisions, unexplainable outputs, hidden AI use |
| **Privacy** | Re-identification, inference attacks, unauthorized profiling |
| **Security** | Adversarial attacks, data poisoning, model theft, prompt injection |
| **Accountability** | Unclear liability, diffuse responsibility, regulatory non-compliance |
| **Environmental** | Energy consumption, carbon footprint, resource utilization |
| **Societal** | Job displacement, misinformation amplification, democratic process interference |
| **Reliability** | Model drift, performance degradation, hallucination, confabulation |

## Annex D: Sector-Specific Considerations

| Sector | Key Considerations |
|--------|-------------------|
| Healthcare | Patient safety, clinical validation, medical device regulations, informed consent |
| Finance | Algorithmic trading, credit scoring fairness, anti-money laundering, explainability for credit decisions |
| Automotive | Safety-critical systems, autonomous driving, real-time decision requirements |
| Public Sector | Citizen rights, democratic oversight, public accountability, non-discrimination |
| Education | Student data privacy, assessment fairness, accessibility |
| Legal | Due process, evidence admissibility, judicial independence |
